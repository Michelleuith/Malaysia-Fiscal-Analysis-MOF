# Strengthening Financial Resilience: MOF Malaysia (2010–2022)

### 📌 Overview
This data storytelling project analyzes 12 years of fiscal data from the Ministry of Finance (MOF) Malaysia. It explores national revenue trends, the economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, and budget alignment with global sustainability goals.

### 🎯 Objective
* Analyze long-term trends in Federal Government Revenue and Expenditure
* Evaluate the fiscal impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on the 2020 budget
* Assess government spending alignment with **Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)**
* Provide data-driven recommendations for future financial resilience

### 🛠 Tools & Technologies
* **Microsoft Excel** (Data Cleaning, Pivot Tables & Interactive Slicers)
* **Data Visualization** (Interactive Dashboards & Sustainability Posters)
* **Data Source:** Ministry of Economy Public Sector Statistics (2010–2022)

### 📊 Key Analysis Performed
* **Fiscal Trend Mapping:** Visualizing the growth of national revenue from RM 159.7B (2010) to its peak in 2019
* **Expenditure Analysis:** Investigating the balance between Operating Expenditure and Development Expenditure
* **Crisis Impact Study:** Quantifying the revenue dip and expenditure spike during the 2020 pandemic year
* **SDG Mapping:** Correlating budget allocations to Education (SDG 4) and Infrastructure (SDG 9)

### 🔍 Key Insights
* 📍 **Revenue Peak:** National revenue reached a record high of RM 264.4B in 2019 before the global pandemic impact
* 📍 **Structural Imbalance:** Operating expenditure consistently consumes a larger share of the budget than development spending
* 📍 **SDG Commitment:** The data confirms high prioritization of Quality Education (SDG 4), reflecting long-term investment in human capital
* 📍 **Recovery Trends:** Identified key fiscal recovery patterns emerging in the post-2020 economic landscape
